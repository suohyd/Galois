    Section 2.3 Field generated by a subset

    We begin by defining an \(F\)-algebra, where \(F\) is a field.

    Definition 2.3.1.

    Let \(F\) be a field. An \(F\)-algebra or an algebra over a field \(F\) is an associative ring \(A\) with unity together with a ring homomorphism \(\varphi\colon F\to Z(A)\text{.}\) The ring \(A\) is also given an \(F\)-vector space structure by defining the scalar multiplication \(\alpha\cdot a\mapsto \varphi(\alpha)a\text{.}\)

    Observation 2.3.2.

    Suppose that \(A\) is an \(F\)-algebra. Then, \(A\) is both a ring and an \(F\)-vector space. Furthermore, the ring and vector space structures are compatible, i.e., for \(\alpha\in F\) and \(a,b\in A\) we have the following.
    \begin{align*} \alpha\cdot(ab) \amp =\varphi(\alpha)(ab)\\ \amp =(\varphi(\alpha)a)b\\ \amp =(\alpha\cdot a)b \end{align*}
    Similarly, we have the following by using the fact that \(\varphi(\alpha)\in Z(A)\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} \alpha\cdot(ab) \amp =\varphi(\alpha)(ab)\\ \amp =(\varphi(\alpha)a)b\\ \amp =(a\varphi(\alpha))b\\ \amp =a(\varphi(\alpha)b)\\ \amp =a(\alpha\cdot b) \end{align*}
    Therefore,
    \begin{equation} \alpha\cdot(ab)=(\alpha\cdot a)b=a(\alpha\cdot b)\tag{2.3.1} \end{equation}

    Observation 2.3.3.

    Note that for \(\alpha\in F\) and \(a\in A\) we have \((\alpha\cdot 1_A)a=\alpha\cdot a\text{.}\) Also, \(a(\alpha\cdot 1_A)=\alpha\cdot a\text{.}\) Hence, \((\alpha\cdot 1_A)a=a(\alpha\cdot 1_A)\text{.}\)

    Example 2.3.4.

    1. Let \(F\) be a field. Then, the polynomial ring \(F[x]\) is an \(F\)-algebra.
    2. The ring of \(n\times n\) matrices with entries in \(F\text{,}\) denoted by \(M_n(F)\text{,}\) is an \(F\)-algebra.
    3. If \(E/F\) is a field extension, then \(E\) is an \(F\)-algebra.

    Definition 2.3.5. Algebra homomorphism.

    Suppose that \(F\) is a field and that \(A,B\) are \(F\)-algebras. An \(F\)-algebra homomorphism from \(A\) to \(B\) is a ring homomorphism as well as an \(F\)-linear map.

    Example 2.3.6. Evaluation as an algebra homomorphism.

    Let \(E/F\) be a field extension, and let \(\alpha\in E\text{.}\) The evaluation map \(\ev_\alpha\colon F[x]\to E\) given by \(p(x)\mapsto p(\alpha)\) is an \(F\)-algebra homomorphism.

    Definition 2.3.7.

    Let \(E/F\) be an extension of fields and \(X\) be a subset of \(E\text{.}\)
    1. The ring generated by \(X\) is the intersection of all subrings of \(E\) containing \(X\text{,}\) denoted by \(F[X]\text{.}\) If \(X=\{a_1,a_2,\cdots,a_n\}\) then, we write \(F[X]=F[a_1,a_2,\cdots,a_n]\text{.}\)
    2. The field generated by \(X\) is the intersection of all subfields of \(E\) containing \(X\text{,}\) denoted by \(F(X)\text{.}\) If \(X=\{a_1,a_2,\cdots,a_n\}\) then, we write \(F(X)=F(a_1,a_2,\cdots,a_n)\text{.}\)

    Proposition 2.3.8.

    Let \(E/F\) be a field extension, and let \(a\in E\text{.}\) Then,
    \begin{equation*} F[a]=\{p(a):p(x)\in F[x]\} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} F(a)=\left\{p(a)/q(a):p(x),q(x)\in F[x],\text{ and }q(a)\neq 0\right\}. \end{equation*}

    Proof.

    Consider the evaluation \(F\)-algebra homomorphism \({\mathrm ev}_a\colon F[x]\to E\) given by \(p(x)\mapsto p(a)\text{.}\) The image of this homomorphism is a subring of \(E\) containing \(a\text{.}\) By the closure of addition and multiplication the image of \({\mathrm ev}_a\) is contained in every subring of \(E\) containing \(F\) and \(a\text{.}\) Thus, the image of \({\mathrm ev}_a\) is the ring generated by \(a\text{,}\) i.e., \(F[a]\text{.}\)
    A similar argument shows the statement about \(F(a)\text{.}\)
